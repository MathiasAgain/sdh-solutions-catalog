Multi-BU Model

Federated Autonomy

Every portfolio company and business unit wants full autonomy over their data. But maturity levels vary dramatically. Our delivery model embraces both realities.

The Challenge

In a multi-BU engagement, you rarely find uniform readiness. One BU may have clean EAN codes and a mature data team; another may run on spreadsheets with inconsistent naming. A one-size-fits-all rollout will either move too slowly for the leaders or overwhelm the laggards.

The Approach

We deploy in maturity-sequenced waves. The highest-maturity BU goes first as the "lighthouse" — proving the model, refining the framework, and generating early ROI. Subsequent BUs onboard in order of readiness, each inheriting a more battle-tested platform.

Mapping Workstream

Matching & Mapping Deep Dive

Expand each workstream below for a detailed breakdown of techniques, steps, and effort.

Product Conformance  (55 – 80 h)
StepDescription
CanonicalizationNormalize product names, strip noise characters, standardize units and packaging descriptors
Deterministic MatchingExact-match on UPC/EAN/GTIN barcodes and known cross-reference codes
Rule-Based MatchingConfigurable business rules for brand + size + flavor combinations, handling known aliases
Fuzzy MatchingLevenshtein, Jaro-Winkler, and token-set-ratio algorithms for near-matches above configurable thresholds
Confidence ScoringEach match pair receives a 0–100 confidence score; below threshold goes to manual review queue
SurvivorshipGolden-record construction: which source attribute wins for each field (brand, category, hierarchy)
Customer Conformance  (20 – 35 h)
StepDescription
Chain / Banner NormalizationStandardize retailer names across sources (e.g., "Wal-Mart" / "Walmart" / "WMT")
Deterministic MatchingExact-match on GLN, DUNS, or internal customer IDs when available
Rule-Based MatchingName + geography + channel rules for known distribution patterns
Fuzzy MatchingAddress-level and name-level fuzzy matching for ship-to / bill-to resolution
Crosswalk TablePersistent source-to-canonical mapping table with override capability and audit trail
Mapping Operations  (12 – 20 h)
StepDescription
Review QueueLow-confidence matches surfaced to business users for approval, with batch review capability
Override MechanismManual overrides persisted to crosswalk with reason codes and expiry dates
Crosswalk PersistenceVersioned mapping tables stored in the data warehouse, queryable for lineage and audit
ReprocessingAbility to re-run matching pipelines when new rules or overrides are added, without data loss

Note: Parts of the mapping operations workflow can be accelerated with purpose-built stewardship tooling, potentially reducing manual effort in the review queue and override steps.

SOW Scope Matrix

Scope, Assumptions & Acceptance

Clear boundary definition to prevent scope creep and align stakeholder expectations.

In Scope

  • Source-system connector build and ingestion pipelines
  • Product and customer matching / conformance logic
  • Unified sales fact table with standard DQ checks
  • Crosswalk tables with override and audit capability
  • Reconciliation reports (source vs. harmonized)
  • Power BI semantic model and starter dashboards (if add-on selected)
  • UAT support and production handover documentation

Out of Scope

  • Source-system changes or remediation at origin
  • ERP or financial system integration
  • Custom ML model training beyond fuzzy matching
  • Ongoing BAU operations post-handover (unless contracted)
  • Organization change management
  • Third-party license procurement
  • Data migration from legacy BI tools

Assumptions

  • Client provides platform access within 5 business days of kick-off
  • Source data is available in agreed-upon format (CSV, API, or DB)
  • Business SME available for 2 – 4 h/week during matching iterations
  • Single cloud tenant (Azure or Databricks) pre-provisioned
  • No more than 50,000 unique SKUs per source in initial scope
  • English-language product and customer data

Acceptance Criteria

  • Reconciliation variance ≤ 1% between source and harmonized totals
  • Product match rate ≥ 92% automated (remaining to review queue)
  • Customer match rate ≥ 88% automated
  • Pipeline SLA: daily refresh completes within 90 minutes
  • DQ scorecard passes all critical-severity rules at go-live
  • UAT sign-off from designated business owner
Risks & Mitigations

Key Risks & Mitigations

Five primary risks identified during pre-engagement analysis, each paired with concrete mitigation actions.

Risk

Source Data Quality Worse Than Expected

Profiling reveals higher-than-anticipated nulls, duplicates, or schema drift, requiring additional cleansing effort.

Mitigation

Early Profiling & Contingency Budget

Phase 0 includes deep profiling. 10% contingency hours built into every estimate. DQ add-on provides automated quarantine for bad records.

Risk

Business SME Availability

Limited access to domain experts delays matching validation and extends timeline.

Mitigation

Structured Review Cadence

Pre-schedule 2h/week SME review slots at kick-off. Batch review queue UI minimizes ad-hoc demands. Escalation path to sponsor if slots are missed.

Risk

Platform Access Delays

IT provisioning, security reviews, or network access takes longer than 5 business days, blocking Phase 1 start.

Mitigation

Parallel Provisioning Track

Submit access requests during Phase 0. Maintain a pre-approved sandbox environment for development. Readiness Scorecard flags this risk early.

Risk

Scope Creep via Unstated Requirements

New sources, KPIs, or audiences surface mid-build, expanding scope beyond agreed tier.

Mitigation

Strict Change Control

SOW scope matrix enforced. Any net-new scope routed through change-request process with impact assessment (hours + timeline + cost delta).

Risk

Low Adoption Post-Launch

Harmonized data is built but not consumed — users revert to legacy reports or manual processes.

Mitigation

Embedded Training & Quick Wins

Starter Pack dashboards designed for day-1 value. Training add-on includes role-based workshops. Success metrics tracked from Phase 1 to demonstrate ROI early.

Prerequisites

Engagement Prerequisites

Seven items to confirm before kick-off, plus gap-severity guidance if any are unmet.

  • Cloud platform access — Tenant, workspace, and service principal credentials provisioned (Azure / Databricks / Snowflake)
  • Source data access — Read access to each in-scope distributor / POS source confirmed with IT and vendor
  • Business sponsor identified — Named executive sponsor with authority to approve scope and sign off UAT
  • SME availability committed — Minimum 2 h/week from product and customer domain experts during matching phases
  • KPI definitions documented — At least draft definitions for primary sales KPIs (revenue, volume, ASP, distribution)
  • Data governance baseline — Existing data classification, retention policies, or PII handling guidelines available
  • Budget approval — Build cost and 12-month run cost approved through procurement / finance

Gap Severity Guide

Green

Minor Gap

+10 – 25 h

Prerequisite partially met. Remediation can happen in parallel with Phase 0 / early Phase 1 without blocking delivery.

Amber

Moderate Gap

+25 – 60 h

Prerequisite not met but path to resolution is clear. Requires dedicated remediation sprint before or during Phase 1.

Red

Critical Gap

+1 – 3+ weeks

Prerequisite entirely unmet. Blocks project start. Requires executive intervention and dedicated remediation workstream.
